## Core Idea 

1. The Big Problem: "The Space Traffic Jam"
Most satellites are just "dumb cameras." They take a massive 100MB photo of the ocean, try to send it to Earth, but the "internet connection" from space is slow and expensive.
The Waste: 99% of that photo is just empty blue water.
The Cost: Sending that "empty" data costs time and a lot of money.

2. Your Solution: The Orbital Scene Preprocessor (OSP)
You built a system that lets the satellite think before it speaks. Instead of sending the whole photo, your AI looks at the photo on the satellite and only sends a tiny text message (1.2KB) back to Earth.
The Result: You reduced the data size by 85,000 times. It’s like sending a text message "I found a ship at these coordinates" instead of mailing a high-def DVD.

3. How It Works (The 3-Step "Magic")
- **Step 1: Super-Vision (Multispectral AI)**
Humans see in 3 colors (Red, Green, Blue). Your AI uses 6 colors, including Infrared.  
Why? Infrared can "see" through light haze and tell the difference between a metal ship and the water, even when it's dark or cloudy.
Tech: You used a modified YOLOv8 (a famous "finding objects" AI) that was shrunk down to fit on a satellite chip.

- **Step 2: The Semantic Gateway (The JSON Brief)**
Once the satellite finds something, it creates a JSON brief.
Think of this as a "Digital Tag." It says: "I found a Vessel, 95% certainty, at Latitude X, Longitude Y."

- **Step 3: The AI Analyst (LLM + RAG)**

On the ground, you have a "Brain" (Gemini 1.5 Pro) waiting.
RAG (The Library): The AI has a library of maritime laws and maps. When it gets the tiny message from the satellite, it checks the library.
Agentic Orchestration: The AI isn't just a chatbot; it's a Boss. If it sees the ship is in a restricted zone, it can automatically "order" the satellite to take a closer look.
